# MODEL RISK & SAFETY COMMITTEE (MRSC) CHARTER

**Ludwig Law Corp LLC**  
**Effective Date:** [Insert Date]  
**Approved By:** Board of Managers  

## 1. PURPOSE AND AUTHORITY

The Model Risk & Safety Committee (MRSC) is established to ensure the safe, ethical, and effective deployment of AI and machine learning models within the Company's legal practice. The MRSC shall oversee model governance, risk management, safety protocols, and compliance with emerging AI regulations across all jurisdictions.

## 2. COMPOSITION

### 2.1 Membership
- Chair: Head of Neuro-Symbolic Systems
- Members: Chief Legal Architect, Chief Compliance & Assurance Officer, external AI ethics expert, and technical leads
- Secretary: Designated by the Chair

### 2.2 Term
Members serve at the pleasure of the Board of Managers.

### 2.3 Expertise
Committee members shall have expertise in AI/ML, risk management, legal ethics, and regulatory compliance.

## 3. RESPONSIBILITIES AND DUTIES

### 3.1 Model Governance
- Oversee AI model development, deployment, and lifecycle management
- Establish model validation and testing protocols
- Review model architecture and training data quality
- Approve major model updates and new deployments

### 3.2 Risk Management
- Assess model risks including bias, hallucinations, and adversarial attacks
- Monitor model performance and drift detection
- Establish risk thresholds and mitigation strategies
- Conduct regular model stress testing and scenario analysis

### 3.3 Safety Protocols
- Develop safety guardrails and fail-safe mechanisms
- Establish incident response procedures for model failures
- Oversee red team testing and adversarial evaluations
- Monitor emerging AI safety research and standards

### 3.4 Regulatory Compliance
- Track AI regulations across jurisdictions (EU AI Act, US AI oversight, etc.)
- Ensure compliance with legal ethics and professional responsibility
- Prepare for regulatory examinations and audits
- Maintain documentation for supervisory review

### 3.5 Performance Monitoring
- Establish KPIs for model accuracy and reliability
- Monitor grounded accuracy (≥ 95% target)
- Track material omission rates (≤ 0.5% target)
- Review model calibration and uncertainty quantification

## 4. MEETINGS AND PROCEDURES

### 4.1 Regular Meetings
- Monthly meetings with bi-weekly technical reviews
- Emergency meetings for critical model issues or incidents

### 4.2 Quorum and Voting
- Quorum: Majority of members
- Decisions: Majority vote for standard approvals, unanimous for critical safety issues

### 4.3 Decision Rights
- Final authority for model deployment and rollback decisions
- Escalation to Board for systemic risk issues or regulatory matters

### 4.4 Documentation
- All model decisions and risk assessments documented
- Incident reports and remediation plans maintained in Evidence Locker

## 5. REPORTING AND OVERSIGHT

### 5.1 Board Reporting
- Monthly model performance and risk dashboard
- Quarterly comprehensive risk assessment
- Immediate notification of material model incidents

### 5.2 Metrics and KPIs
- Grounded accuracy: ≥ 95%
- Material omission rate: ≤ 0.5%
- Incident response time: < 4 hours
- Regulatory compliance: 100%

### 5.3 Annual Review
- Model governance effectiveness assessment
- Technology and regulatory landscape review
- Charter updates and process improvements

## 6. RESOURCES AND SUPPORT

### 6.1 Technical Team
- Dedicated AI safety and risk management team
- Access to model monitoring and testing infrastructure
- External consultants for specialized expertise

### 6.2 Technology
- Automated model monitoring and alerting systems
- Red team testing platforms
- Regulatory compliance tracking tools

### 6.3 Training
- AI ethics and safety training for all technical staff
- Regulatory awareness programs
- Incident response drills

## 7. AMENDMENT AND REVIEW

This Charter may be amended by the Board of Managers. The Charter shall be reviewed annually and updated to reflect technological advancements and regulatory changes.

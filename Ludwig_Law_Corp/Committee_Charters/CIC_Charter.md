# CONFLICTS & INDEPENDENCE COMMITTEE (CIC) CHARTER

**Ludwig Law Corp LLC**  
**Effective Date:** [Insert Date]  
**Approved By:** Board of Managers  

## 1. PURPOSE AND AUTHORITY

The Conflicts & Independence Committee (CIC) is established to ensure the Company's compliance with legal and ethical standards regarding conflicts of interest, attorney independence, client confidentiality, and professional responsibility. The CIC shall maintain the integrity of the Company's single-firm conflicts perimeter and oversee all matters related to client acceptance, representation, and information barriers.

## 2. COMPOSITION

### 2.1 Membership
- Chair: Appointed by the Board of Managers
- Members: General Counsel (ex officio), Chief Compliance & Assurance Officer (ex officio), and 2-3 additional members appointed by the Board
- Secretary: Designated by the Chair

### 2.2 Term
Members serve at the pleasure of the Board of Managers.

### 2.3 Independence
Committee members must be free from material conflicts that could impair their judgment.

## 3. RESPONSIBILITIES AND DUTIES

### 3.1 Conflicts Management
- Maintain and administer the global conflicts system
- Review and clear potential conflicts for all engagements
- Establish and enforce information barriers and screens
- Monitor compliance with conflicts policies

### 3.2 Client Acceptance
- Review client acceptance criteria and procedures
- Evaluate sensitive matters involving sovereign clients, state-owned entities, and politically exposed persons
- Assess reputational and regulatory risks

### 3.3 Affiliate Parity
- Ensure consistent treatment of affiliates within the single-firm perimeter
- Review cross-entity referrals and collaborations
- Maintain parity in conflicts screening across jurisdictions

### 3.4 Independence Standards
- Oversee attorney independence requirements
- Review lateral hires and their conflicts profiles
- Monitor compliance with professional conduct rules

### 3.5 Policy Development
- Develop and update conflicts policies and procedures
- Establish escalation thresholds and approval matrices
- Create training programs for conflicts awareness

## 4. MEETINGS AND PROCEDURES

### 4.1 Regular Meetings
- Monthly meetings, or more frequently as needed
- Special meetings called by Chair or upon request of any member

### 4.2 Quorum and Voting
- Quorum: Majority of members
- Decisions: Majority vote
- Recusal: Members must recuse themselves from matters involving personal conflicts

### 4.3 Decision Rights
- Final authority for conflicts clearances up to defined thresholds
- Escalation to Board for matters exceeding thresholds or involving strategic clients

### 4.4 Documentation
- All decisions and rationales shall be documented
- Minutes maintained in the Evidence Locker

## 5. REPORTING AND OVERSIGHT

### 5.1 Board Reporting
- Quarterly reports to the Board of Managers
- Immediate notification of material conflicts issues or regulatory inquiries

### 5.2 Metrics and KPIs
- Conflicts clearance processing time (< 24 hours for standard matters)
- Escalation rate (< 1 per 1,000 engagements)
- Zero regulator findings related to conflicts

### 5.3 Annual Review
- Self-assessment of committee effectiveness
- Policy updates and training refreshers

## 6. RESOURCES AND SUPPORT

### 6.1 Staff Support
- Dedicated conflicts team reporting to the CIC Secretary
- Access to global conflicts database and screening tools

### 6.2 Technology
- AI-assisted conflicts prediction and screening
- Automated alerts for potential conflicts

### 6.3 Training
- Mandatory annual training for all attorneys and staff
- Specialized training for committee members

## 7. AMENDMENT AND REVIEW

This Charter may be amended by the Board of Managers. The Charter shall be reviewed annually and updated as necessary to reflect changes in law, regulation, or Company operations.

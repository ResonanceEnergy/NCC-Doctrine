# PRICING, PROFITABILITY & PORTFOLIO COMMITTEE (P3C) CHARTER

**Ludwig Law Corp LLC**  
**Effective Date:** [Insert Date]  
**Approved By:** Board of Managers  

## 1. PURPOSE AND AUTHORITY

The Pricing, Profitability & Portfolio Committee (P3C) is established to optimize the Company's financial performance through disciplined pricing, profitability management, and strategic portfolio allocation. The P3C shall ensure pricing integrity, drive RPL uplift, and allocate resources to high-value opportunities while maintaining competitive positioning.

## 2. COMPOSITION

### 2.1 Membership
- Chair: Chief Financial Officer or designated senior partner
- Members: Chief Legal Architect, Head of Neuro-Symbolic Systems, Regional Managing Partners, and 1-2 external advisors
- Secretary: Designated by the Chair

### 2.2 Term
Members serve at the pleasure of the Board of Managers.

### 2.3 Expertise
Committee members shall have expertise in finance, pricing strategy, and market economics.

## 3. RESPONSIBILITIES AND DUTIES

### 3.1 Pricing Strategy
- Establish and maintain global pricing bands and rate cards
- Approve alternative fee arrangements (AFAs) and success fees
- Monitor market rates and competitive positioning
- Develop pricing models for AI-assisted services

### 3.2 Profitability Management
- Set RPL uplift targets (8-15% annually)
- Monitor realization rates (target ≥ 92%)
- Review matter profitability and write-downs
- Establish profitability guardrails and early warning systems

### 3.3 Portfolio Management
- Allocate resources across practice areas and geographies
- Evaluate sector and client portfolio mix
- Assess strategic investments and market entry decisions
- Monitor cross-sell opportunities and client development

### 3.4 Economic Analysis
- Analyze matter economics and leverage metrics
- Review AI impact on productivity and pricing
- Assess lateral hire economics and book durability
- Forecast revenue and profitability trends

## 4. MEETINGS AND PROCEDURES

### 4.1 Regular Meetings
- Monthly meetings with quarterly deep-dive sessions
- Special meetings for urgent pricing or portfolio decisions

### 4.2 Quorum and Voting
- Quorum: Majority of members
- Decisions: Majority vote for standard matters, supermajority for strategic changes

### 4.3 Decision Rights
- Final authority for pricing band adjustments and matter-specific approvals
- Escalation to Board for major portfolio shifts or compensation policy changes

### 4.4 Documentation
- All pricing decisions and rationales documented
- Economic analysis maintained in centralized repository

## 5. REPORTING AND OVERSIGHT

### 5.1 Board Reporting
- Monthly financial dashboards
- Quarterly profitability and portfolio reviews
- Annual strategic pricing plan

### 5.2 Metrics and KPIs
- RPL growth: +8-15% YoY
- Realization rate: ≥ 92%
- Pricing discipline: < 5% exceptions requiring approval
- Portfolio concentration risk: Within defined limits

### 5.3 Annual Review
- Effectiveness assessment and process improvements
- Market benchmarking and competitive analysis

## 6. RESOURCES AND SUPPORT

### 6.1 Analytics Team
- Dedicated pricing and profitability analysts
- Access to financial systems and matter management data

### 6.2 Technology
- AI-powered pricing optimization tools
- Real-time profitability dashboards
- Predictive analytics for matter economics

### 6.3 Training
- Pricing strategy training for partners and business development teams
- Economic analysis workshops

## 7. AMENDMENT AND REVIEW

This Charter may be amended by the Board of Managers. The Charter shall be reviewed annually and updated to reflect market conditions and Company strategy.

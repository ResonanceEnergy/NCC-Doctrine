# LATERAL & TALENT ECONOMICS COMMITTEE (LTEC) CHARTER

**Ludwig Law Corp LLC**  
**Effective Date:** [Insert Date]  
**Approved By:** Board of Managers  

## 1. PURPOSE AND AUTHORITY

The Lateral & Talent Economics Committee (LTEC) is established to optimize the Company's human capital strategy through disciplined lateral hiring, talent development, and compensation alignment. The LTEC shall ensure the recruitment and retention of top talent while maintaining economic discipline and cultural alignment with the Company's AI-first mission.

## 2. COMPOSITION

### 2.1 Membership
- Chair: Designated senior partner with HR expertise
- Members: Regional Managing Partners, Chief Legal Architect, Head of Neuro-Symbolic Systems, and HR lead
- Secretary: Designated by the Chair

### 2.2 Term
Members serve at the pleasure of the Board of Managers.

### 2.3 Expertise
Committee members shall have expertise in talent acquisition, compensation design, and organizational development.

## 3. RESPONSIBILITIES AND DUTIES

### 3.1 Lateral Hiring Strategy
- Establish criteria and processes for lateral partner hiring
- Review lateral candidate pipelines and market intelligence
- Assess book durability and economic viability of lateral hires
- Monitor integration success and retention metrics

### 3.2 Compensation Design
- Design compensation bands and incentive structures
- Align compensation with AI adoption and leverage metrics
- Establish strategic premiums for key practice areas
- Monitor market competitiveness and internal equity

### 3.3 Talent Development
- Oversee training and development programs
- Promote AI literacy and technical skills development
- Establish career progression frameworks
- Monitor diversity, equity, and inclusion initiatives

### 3.4 Economic Analysis
- Analyze lateral hire economics and ROI
- Track book realization and productivity metrics
- Assess churn drivers and retention strategies
- Forecast talent needs and succession planning

### 3.5 Cultural Alignment
- Ensure alignment with AI-first culture and values
- Review onboarding processes for technical and cultural fit
- Monitor employee engagement and satisfaction
- Establish performance management frameworks

## 4. MEETINGS AND PROCEDURES

### 4.1 Regular Meetings
- Monthly meetings with quarterly strategic sessions
- Special meetings for significant hiring decisions

### 4.2 Quorum and Voting
- Quorum: Majority of members
- Decisions: Majority vote for standard approvals, supermajority for compensation policy changes

### 4.3 Decision Rights
- Final authority for lateral hiring approvals within established criteria
- Escalation to Board for strategic hires or compensation policy changes

### 4.4 Documentation
- All hiring decisions and economic analyses documented
- Compensation decisions maintained with rationale
- Performance metrics tracked and reviewed

## 5. REPORTING AND OVERSIGHT

### 5.1 Board Reporting
- Monthly talent acquisition and retention dashboard
- Quarterly compensation and economic analysis
- Annual talent strategy review

This Charter may be amended by the Board of Managers. The Charter shall be reviewed annually and updated to reflect market conditions and Company strategy.

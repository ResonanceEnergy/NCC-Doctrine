# LUDWIG LAW CORP LLC — OPERATING AGREEMENT (V1.0)

**Effective Date:** [Insert Date]  
**Jurisdiction:** Delaware Limited Liability Company Act  
**Form:** Manager-Managed LLC with Integrated Profit Pool  

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Members desire to form a limited liability company for the purpose of providing legal and advisory services worldwide, operating AI-enabled legal products, holding and licensing intellectual property, and managing subsidiaries and affiliates;

WHEREAS, the Members intend to create a unified, AI-driven legal enterprise that integrates insights from top global law firms while maintaining the highest standards of compliance, independence, and client service;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, the Members agree as follows:

## ARTICLE I — ORGANIZATION AND PURPOSE

### Section 1.1 Formation
Ludwig Law Corp LLC (the "Company") is formed as a Delaware limited liability company pursuant to the Delaware Limited Liability Company Act (6 Del. C. § 18-101 et seq.) and this Operating Agreement (the "Agreement").

### Section 1.2 Name
The name of the Company shall be Ludwig Law Corp LLC.

### Section 1.3 Principal Place of Business
The principal place of business of the Company shall be as designated by the Board of Managers from time to time.

### Section 1.4 Registered Agent
The registered agent of the Company shall be as designated by the Board of Managers.

### Section 1.5 Term
The term of the Company shall be perpetual unless dissolved pursuant to Article X hereof.

### Section 1.6 Purpose
The Company shall engage in the following business activities:
(a) Provide legal and advisory services worldwide;
(b) Develop, operate, and license AI-enabled legal products and services;
(c) Hold, develop, and license intellectual property;
(d) Manage subsidiaries, affiliates, and related entities;
(e) Engage in any lawful act or activity related to the foregoing.

### Section 1.7 Powers
The Company shall have all powers necessary or convenient to carry out its purposes, including the power to:
(a) Enter into contracts and agreements;
(b) Acquire, own, and dispose of property;
(c) Borrow money and incur indebtedness;
(d) Issue securities and equity interests;
(e) Form subsidiaries and affiliates;
(f) Conduct business in any jurisdiction.

## ARTICLE II — MEMBERS AND CAPITAL

### Section 2.1 Members
The initial Members of the Company shall be [Insert Names]. Additional Members may be admitted pursuant to Article IX.

### Section 2.2 Capital Contributions
(a) Initial Capital Contributions: Each Member shall contribute capital as set forth in Exhibit A.
(b) Additional Contributions: The Board of Managers may require additional capital contributions as needed.
(c) Capital Accounts: The Company shall maintain Capital Accounts for each Member in accordance with Treas. Reg. § 1.731-1(a)(1)(ii).

### Section 2.3 Distributions
(a) Quarterly Distributions: Subject to available cash and reserves, distributions shall be made quarterly.
(b) Interim Distributions: The Board may authorize interim distributions.
(c) Waterfall: Distributions shall follow the waterfall set forth in Section 3.3.

### Section 2.4 No Interest
No interest shall accrue on capital contributions or Capital Account balances.

## ARTICLE III — MANAGEMENT AND GOVERNANCE

### Section 3.1 Manager-Managed
The Company shall be manager-managed. Management authority is vested in the Board of Managers.

### Section 3.2 Board of Managers
(a) Composition: The Board shall consist of 5-7 Managers, appointed by the Members.
(b) Term: Managers serve at the pleasure of the Members.
(c) Powers: The Board shall have full authority to manage the Company's business and affairs.
(d) Meetings: The Board shall meet quarterly, or more frequently as needed.
(e) Quorum: A majority of Managers shall constitute a quorum.
(f) Voting: Decisions require a majority vote, except as otherwise provided.

### Section 3.3 Officers
The Board shall appoint the following officers:
(a) Chief Legal Architect
(b) General Counsel
(c) Chief Compliance & Assurance Officer
(d) Head of Neuro-Symbolic Systems
(e) Chief Financial Officer
(f) Such other officers as the Board deems necessary.

### Section 3.4 Committees
The Board shall establish and maintain the following committees:
(a) Conflicts & Independence Committee (CIC)
(b) Pricing, Profitability & Portfolio Committee (P3C)
(c) Model Risk & Safety Committee (MRSC)
(d) Audit & Evidence Locker Committee (AELC)
(e) Lateral & Talent Economics Committee (LTEC)

Each committee shall have a charter defining its authority, responsibilities, and procedures.

## ARTICLE IV — COMPENSATION AND PROFIT ALLOCATION

### Section 4.1 Integrated Profit Pool
All revenues of the Company and its subsidiaries shall accrue to the Company. Operating subsidiaries shall settle intercompany charges on an arm's-length basis.

### Section 4.2 Compensation
(a) The Board shall establish annual compensation bands for Members and employees.
(b) Compensation shall be based on scorecards including origination, execution, cross-sell, accuracy, AI leverage, realization, and RPL uplift.
(c) Strategic premiums shall apply for key sectors and products.

### Section 4.3 Price Discipline
(a) The P3C shall approve pricing bands and policies.
(b) Engagements outside approved bands require P3C pre-approval.

## ARTICLE V — CONFLICTS, INDEPENDENCE AND CLIENT ACCEPTANCE

### Section 5.1 Single Perimeter
All Ludwig entities and affiliates shall be treated as a single firm for conflicts, confidentiality, and independence purposes.

### Section 5.2 Screening Requirements
(a) CIC clearance is required for engagements involving:
   (i) Directly adverse parties;
   (ii) Sovereign/state-owned counterparties;
   (iii) Material fact overlap with existing matters.
(b) Information barriers may be imposed as needed.

### Section 5.3 Client Acceptance
The CIC shall establish policies for client acceptance and continued representation.

## ARTICLE VI — AI GOVERNANCE, SAFETY AND EVIDENCE

### Section 6.1 Verification Standards
All AI-generated analysis must include:
(a) Source citations with version IDs;
(b) Jurisdictional markers;
(c) Uncertainty thresholds with escalation protocols.

### Section 6.2 Evidence Locker
(a) Immutable logs shall be maintained for all AI processes.
(b) Retention policies shall comply with applicable laws.

### Section 6.3 Model Lifecycle
The MRSC shall oversee model evaluation, testing, and rollback procedures.

## ARTICLE VII — COMPLIANCE AND ASSURANCE

### Section 7.1 Controls-as-Code
All regulatory obligations shall be mapped to controls, owners, evidence, and tests in the Regulus library.

### Section 7.2 Assurance Program
(a) Quarterly internal audits;
(b) Annual external assurance;
(c) Board risk dashboard maintained by the CCA.

### Section 7.3 Incident Response
Notifiable incidents shall be reported within 24 hours with remediation plans tracked to closure.

## ARTICLE VIII — DATA, PRIVACY AND SECURITY

### Section 8.1 Data Zoning
(a) Privilege zones shall segregate confidential information.
(b) Client data residency requirements shall be observed.

### Section 8.2 Security Standards
Zero-trust access, encryption, and confidential computing shall be maintained.

## ARTICLE IX — ADMISSION, WITHDRAWAL AND TRANSFERS

### Section 9.1 Admission of New Members
New Members require 2/3 Board approval following conflicts and cultural diligence.

### Section 9.2 Withdrawal
Members may withdraw with 180 days' notice, subject to non-solicit and stewardship provisions.

### Section 9.3 Transfers
Transfers are restricted with right of first offer to the Company and existing Members.

## ARTICLE X — DISSOLUTION AND WINDING UP

### Section 10.1 Dissolution Triggers
The Company may be dissolved by:
(a) Supermajority Board vote;
(b) Unlawful activity;
(c) Court order or operation of law.

### Section 10.2 Winding Up
Dissolution shall be overseen by the AELC with distributions following the capital waterfall.

## ARTICLE XI — MISCELLANEOUS

### Section 11.1 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by Delaware law.

### Section 11.2 Amendments
Amendments require Board approval.

### Section 11.3 Severability
Invalid provisions shall not affect the remainder.

### Section 11.4 Entire Agreement
This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding among the Members.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the Members have executed this Agreement as of the date first above written.

# NNO Compartmentalization Doctrine
**Need-to-Know Only Compartmentalization Doctrine**

**Version:** 1.0.0 | **Classification:** NCC TOP SECRET | **Date:** 2026-01-28

## Executive Summary
The NNO (Need-to-Know Only) Compartmentalization Doctrine establishes the fundamental principle that information access is restricted to only those individuals who require specific knowledge to perform their assigned duties. This doctrine ensures operational security, prevents unauthorized information dissemination, and maintains the integrity of NCC's global operations.

## Core Principles

### 1. Need-to-Know Foundation
- **Access Limitation:** Personnel receive only the information necessary for their current and planned duties
- **Compartmentalization:** Information is divided into secure compartments with controlled access
- **Zero Trust:** All access requests are verified and justified before approval

### 2. Information Classification Hierarchy
- **NCC TOP SECRET:** Strategic corporate intelligence, quantum algorithms, global operations
- **NCC SECRET:** Subsidiary operations, financial data, proprietary technologies
- **NCC CONFIDENTIAL:** Internal communications, project details, personnel data
- **NCC RESTRICTED:** General business information, public partnerships
- **NCC UNCLASSIFIED:** Public information, marketing materials

### 3. Compartment Structure
- **Corporate Level:** CEO, C-Suite (Alpha Team)
- **Subsidiary Level:** Division leads, key executives
- **Operational Level:** Department heads, project managers
- **Task Level:** Individual contributors, specialized teams

### Compartmentalization Rules
1. **Single Responsibility:** Each compartment serves one primary function
2. **Minimal Overlap:** Compartments share only essential interfaces
3. **Air-Gapped Systems:** Critical compartments maintain physical separation
4. **Need-to-Know Verification:** All access requires documented justification
5. **Regular Audits:** Compartment integrity reviewed quarterly

## NCC-Wide Application

### Corporate Governance
- Board communications compartmentalized by committee
- Executive decisions isolated to relevant stakeholders
- Strategic planning restricted to authorized personnel

### Subsidiary Operations
- Each subsidiary operates in isolated compartment
- Cross-subsidiary collaboration requires formal approval
- Technology transfer controlled through secure channels

### Global Operations
- Regional operations maintain local autonomy
- International data flows require encryption and approval
- Cross-border activities compartmentalized by jurisdiction

### Technology & Innovation
- R&D projects isolated until commercialization
- AI/ML models compartmentalized by application domain
- Quantum computing research restricted to authorized facilities

## Security Protocols

### Access Management
- **Multi-Factor Authentication:** Required for all compartment access
- **Biometric Verification:** For high-security compartments
- **Session Monitoring:** All access logged and audited
- **Automatic Revocation:** Access removed upon role change or termination

### Information Handling
- **Encryption Standards:** AES-256 minimum for all classified data
- **Data Loss Prevention:** Automated monitoring and blocking
- **Clean Desk Policy:** Physical documents secured when unattended
- **Remote Work Controls:** VPN required, screen monitoring active

### Incident Response
- **Breach Notification:** Immediate escalation to Security Council
- **Compartment Lockdown:** Affected compartments isolated automatically
- **Damage Assessment:** Independent audit within 24 hours
- **Recovery Protocols:** Secure restoration with enhanced controls

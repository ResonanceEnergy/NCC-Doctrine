# AUDIT & EVIDENCE LOCKER COMMITTEE (AELC) CHARTER

**Ludwig Law Corp LLC**  
**Effective Date:** [Insert Date]  
**Approved By:** Board of Managers  

## 1. PURPOSE AND AUTHORITY

The Audit & Evidence Locker Committee (AELC) is established to ensure the integrity, auditability, and reconstructability of all Company processes, particularly those involving AI systems and legal advice. The AELC shall maintain immutable evidence trails, oversee audit processes, and ensure compliance with record-keeping and evidentiary standards.

## 2. COMPOSITION

### 2.1 Membership
- Chair: Chief Compliance & Assurance Officer
- Members: General Counsel, Head of Neuro-Symbolic Systems, external audit expert, and IT security lead
- Secretary: Designated by the Chair

### 2.2 Term
Members serve at the pleasure of the Board of Managers.

### 2.3 Expertise
Committee members shall have expertise in audit, compliance, information security, and evidence management.

## 3. RESPONSIBILITIES AND DUTIES

### 3.1 Evidence Locker Management
- Oversee the design and operation of the Evidence Locker system
- Establish retention policies for different types of evidence
- Ensure immutability and chain-of-custody for critical records
- Maintain secure access controls and audit trails

### 3.2 Audit Oversight
- Coordinate internal and external audit activities
- Review audit findings and remediation plans
- Establish audit protocols for AI systems and legal processes
- Monitor compliance with audit standards and regulations

### 3.3 Reconstructability Assurance
- Ensure all legal advice and AI outputs can be reconstructed
- Maintain version control for models, data, and processes
- Establish testing protocols for evidence integrity
- Oversee disaster recovery and business continuity for evidence systems

### 3.4 Regulatory Compliance
- Prepare audit kits for regulatory examinations
- Maintain compliance with record-keeping requirements
- Coordinate with external auditors and regulators
- Establish protocols for evidence production in legal proceedings

### 3.5 Quality Assurance
- Conduct periodic testing of evidence systems
- Review sample matters for reconstructability
- Monitor data integrity and backup procedures
- Establish metrics for evidence quality and accessibility

## 4. MEETINGS AND PROCEDURES

### 4.1 Regular Meetings
- Quarterly meetings with monthly subcommittee reviews
- Special meetings for audit findings or system incidents

### 4.2 Quorum and Voting
- Quorum: Majority of members
- Decisions: Majority vote for standard matters, supermajority for policy changes

### 4.3 Decision Rights
- Final authority for evidence retention and audit procedures
- Escalation to Board for material findings or systemic issues

### 4.4 Documentation
- All audit activities and findings documented
- Evidence Locker access logs maintained
- Remediation plans tracked to completion

## 5. REPORTING AND OVERSIGHT

### 5.1 Board Reporting
- Quarterly audit status and findings report
- Annual assurance opinion and recommendations
- Immediate notification of material audit issues

### 5.2 Metrics and KPIs
- Reconstructability rate: 100% for sampled matters
- Evidence integrity: Zero breaches or tampering incidents
- Audit finding resolution: < 30 days average
- Regulatory exam readiness: 100%

### 5.3 Annual Review
- Audit program effectiveness assessment
- Technology and process improvements
- Regulatory requirement updates

## 6. RESOURCES AND SUPPORT

### 6.1 Audit Team
- Dedicated internal audit function
- Access to external audit firms and consultants
- Forensic technology and evidence analysis tools

### 6.2 Technology
- Immutable evidence storage systems
- Automated audit and monitoring tools
- Secure evidence production platforms

### 6.3 Training
- Audit methodology and evidence handling training
- Regulatory compliance workshops
- Technology security awareness programs

## 7. AMENDMENT AND REVIEW

This Charter may be amended by the Board of Managers. The Charter shall be reviewed annually and updated to reflect technological and regulatory changes.

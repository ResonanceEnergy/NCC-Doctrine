# FARADAY FINANCIAL CORPORATION LLC — OPERATING AGREEMENT (V1.0)

**Effective Date:** [Insert Date]  
**Jurisdiction:** Delaware Limited Liability Company Act  
**Form:** Manager-Managed LLC with Integrated Performance Pool  

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Members desire to form a limited liability company for the purpose of quantitative trading, investment management, and related financial activities worldwide;

WHEREAS, the Members intend to create a world-class quantitative investment firm that integrates advanced AI with traditional quantitative strategies while maintaining the highest standards of risk management and regulatory compliance;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, the Members agree as follows:

## ARTICLE I — ORGANIZATION AND PURPOSE

### Section 1.1 Formation
Faraday Financial Corporation LLC (the "Company") is formed as a Delaware limited liability company pursuant to the Delaware Limited Liability Company Act (6 Del. C. § 18-101 et seq.) and this Operating Agreement (the "Agreement").

### Section 1.2 Name
The name of the Company shall be Faraday Financial Corporation LLC.

### Section 1.3 Principal Place of Business
The principal place of business of the Company shall be as designated by the Board of Managers from time to time.

### Section 1.4 Registered Agent
The registered agent of the Company shall be as designated by the Board of Managers.

### Section 1.5 Term
The term of the Company shall be perpetual unless dissolved pursuant to Article VIII hereof.

### Section 1.6 Purpose
The Company shall engage in the following business activities:
(a) Quantitative trading and investment management;
(b) Development and deployment of algorithmic trading strategies;
(c) Risk management and portfolio optimization;
(d) Financial research and analysis;
(e) Any lawful act or activity related to the foregoing.

### Section 1.7 Powers
The Company shall have all powers necessary or convenient to carry out its purposes, including the power to:
(a) Enter into trading agreements and contracts;
(b) Acquire and dispose of securities and financial instruments;
(c) Borrow money and incur indebtedness;
(d) Form subsidiaries and affiliates;
(e) Conduct business in any jurisdiction subject to regulatory requirements.

## ARTICLE II — MEMBERS AND CAPITAL

### Section 2.1 Members
The initial Members of the Company shall be [Insert Names]. Additional Members may be admitted pursuant to Article VII.

### Section 2.2 Capital Contributions
(a) Initial Capital Contributions: Each Member shall contribute capital as set forth in Exhibit A.
(b) Additional Contributions: The Board of Managers may require additional capital contributions as needed for trading activities.
(c) Capital Accounts: The Company shall maintain Capital Accounts for each Member in accordance with Treas. Reg. § 1.731-1(a)(1)(ii).

### Section 2.3 Distributions
(a) Quarterly Distributions: Subject to available profits and regulatory requirements, distributions shall be made quarterly.
(b) Interim Distributions: The Board may authorize interim distributions with regulatory approval.
(c) Waterfall: Distributions shall follow the waterfall set forth in Section 3.3.

### Section 2.4 No Interest
No interest shall accrue on capital contributions or Capital Account balances.

## ARTICLE III — MANAGEMENT AND GOVERNANCE

### Section 3.1 Manager-Managed
The Company shall be manager-managed. Management authority is vested in the Board of Managers.

### Section 3.2 Board of Managers
(a) Composition: The Board shall consist of 7-9 Managers, appointed by the Members.
(b) Term: Managers serve at the pleasure of the Members.
(c) Powers: The Board shall have full authority to manage the Company's business and affairs.
(d) Meetings: The Board shall meet quarterly, or more frequently as needed.
(e) Quorum: A majority of Managers shall constitute a quorum.
(f) Voting: Decisions require a majority vote, except as otherwise provided.

### Section 3.3 Officers
The Board shall appoint the following officers:
(a) Chief Investment Officer
(b) Chief Risk Officer
(c) Chief Technology Officer
(d) Head of Quantitative Research
(e) Chief Financial Officer
(f) Such other officers as the Board deems necessary.

### Section 3.4 Committees
The Board shall establish and maintain the following committees:
(a) Investment Committee (IC)
(b) Risk Management Committee (RMC)
(c) Model Validation Committee (MVC)
(d) Capital Allocation Committee (CAC)
(e) Technology & Data Committee (TDC)

Each committee shall have a charter defining its authority, responsibilities, and procedures.

## ARTICLE IV — COMPENSATION AND PERFORMANCE ALLOCATION

### Section 4.1 Performance Pool
All trading profits and losses accrue to the Company. Individual strategy performance is tracked for compensation purposes.

### Section 4.2 Compensation
(a) The Board shall establish annual compensation bands based on risk-adjusted performance.
(b) Compensation shall be based on Sharpe ratio contribution, alpha generation, and risk management effectiveness.
(c) Team-based incentives shall apply for collective performance.

### Section 4.3 Performance Incentives
(a) Compensation tied to multi-year performance metrics.
(b) Clawback provisions for performance restatements or regulatory violations.

## ARTICLE V — RISK MANAGEMENT AND COMPLIANCE

### Section 5.1 Risk Limits
(a) The RMC shall establish position limits, VaR thresholds, and leverage constraints.
(b) All trading activities subject to pre-trade and real-time risk controls.

### Section 5.2 Regulatory Compliance
(a) The Company shall maintain all required registrations and licenses.
(b) All activities subject to applicable securities laws and regulations.
(c) Regular regulatory reporting and examinations.

### Section 5.3 Model Validation
The MVC shall oversee the development, testing, and deployment of all quantitative models and strategies.

## ARTICLE VI — TECHNOLOGY AND DATA GOVERNANCE

### Section 6.1 Trading Infrastructure
(a) The TDC shall oversee all trading systems, execution platforms, and connectivity.
(b) Redundant systems and disaster recovery protocols required.

### Section 6.2 Data Management
(a) All market data, proprietary signals, and research subject to quality controls.
(b) Data security and privacy requirements maintained.

### Section 6.3 Intellectual Property
All models, algorithms, and strategies developed by the Company are proprietary assets.

## ARTICLE VII — ADMISSION, WITHDRAWAL AND TRANSFERS

### Section 7.1 Admission of New Members
New Members require Board approval following quantitative expertise and risk culture assessment.

### Section 7.2 Withdrawal
Members may withdraw with 180 days' notice, subject to performance obligations and clawback provisions.

### Section 7.3 Transfers
Transfers are restricted with right of first offer to the Company and existing Members.

## ARTICLE VIII — DISSOLUTION AND WINDING UP

### Section 8.1 Dissolution Triggers
The Company may be dissolved by:
(a) Supermajority Board vote;
(b) Regulatory requirement;
(c) Insolvency or inability to meet obligations.

### Section 8.2 Winding Up
Dissolution shall be overseen by the RMC with orderly position unwinding and asset distribution.

## ARTICLE IX — MISCELLANEOUS

### Section 9.1 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by Delaware law.

### Section 9.2 Amendments
Amendments require Board approval.

### Section 9.3 Severability
Invalid provisions shall not affect the remainder.

### Section 9.4 Entire Agreement
This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding among the Members.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the Members have executed this Agreement as of the date first above written.

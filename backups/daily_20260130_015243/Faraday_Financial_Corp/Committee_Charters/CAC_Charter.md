# CAPITAL ALLOCATION COMMITTEE (CAC) CHARTER

**Faraday Financial Corporation LLC**  
**Effective Date:** [Insert Date]  
**Approved By:** Board of Managers  

## 1. PURPOSE AND AUTHORITY

The Capital Allocation Committee (CAC) is established to optimize the deployment and management of the Company's capital across trading strategies, risk management activities, and growth initiatives. The CAC shall ensure efficient capital utilization while maintaining liquidity, managing leverage, and maximizing risk-adjusted returns.

## 2. COMPOSITION

### 2.1 Membership
- Chair: Chief Financial Officer
- Members: Chief Investment Officer, Chief Risk Officer, Head of Quantitative Research, and treasury lead
- Secretary: Designated by the Chair

### 2.2 Term
Members serve at the pleasure of the Board of Managers.

### 2.3 Expertise
Committee members shall have expertise in finance, capital markets, risk management, and quantitative analysis.

## 3. RESPONSIBILITIES AND DUTIES

### 3.1 Capital Planning
- Develop long-term capital requirements and growth plans
- Assess capital adequacy for current and planned activities
- Monitor regulatory capital requirements and ratios
- Plan capital raising activities and shareholder distributions

### 3.2 Leverage Management
- Establish leverage limits and borrowing policies
- Monitor leverage ratios across strategies and portfolios
- Assess funding costs and liquidity requirements
- Manage margin and collateral requirements

### 3.3 Liquidity Management
- Maintain adequate liquidity buffers and contingency funding
- Monitor cash flows and working capital requirements
- Assess market liquidity and funding market conditions
- Develop liquidity stress testing scenarios

### 3.4 Performance Attribution
- Analyze capital efficiency and return on allocated capital
- Assess strategy contribution to overall capital returns
- Monitor capital velocity and utilization rates
- Optimize capital allocation across opportunity set

### 3.5 Risk-Adjusted Capital Allocation
- Balance risk and return in capital deployment decisions
- Assess capital consumption by risk type and strategy
- Monitor concentration risks and diversification benefits
- Adjust allocations based on changing market conditions

## 4. MEETINGS AND PROCEDURES

### 4.1 Regular Meetings
- Weekly capital position and liquidity review
- Monthly capital allocation and performance analysis
- Quarterly strategic capital planning sessions

### 4.2 Quorum and Voting
- Quorum: Majority of members
- Decisions: Majority vote for standard allocation decisions, supermajority for major capital changes

### 4.3 Decision Rights
- Authority to adjust leverage and capital allocations within approved limits
- Power to approve capital raising and distribution decisions
- Escalation to Board for strategic capital changes or funding crises

### 4.4 Documentation
- All capital decisions and allocation rationales documented
- Liquidity and leverage positions tracked
- Performance attribution analyses maintained

## 5. REPORTING AND OVERSIGHT

### 5.1 Board Reporting
- Weekly capital and liquidity dashboard
- Monthly capital allocation and efficiency report
- Quarterly strategic capital plan and funding outlook

### 5.2 Metrics and KPIs
- Return on allocated capital: >25% annualized target
- Leverage ratio: Within approved ranges (3-8x)
- Liquidity coverage: >20% buffer maintained
- Capital efficiency: Optimal risk-adjusted deployment

### 5.3 Annual Review
- Capital allocation strategy effectiveness assessment
- Market condition analysis and capital market access
- Process improvements and technology enhancements

## 6. RESOURCES AND SUPPORT

### 6.1 Finance Team
- Dedicated capital management and treasury team
- Access to capital markets and banking relationships
- Financial modeling and capital planning tools

### 6.2 Technology
- Real-time capital and liquidity monitoring systems
- Risk-adjusted return optimization platforms
- Capital allocation and performance attribution tools

### 6.3 Training
- Capital management and treasury operations training
- Risk-adjusted capital allocation methodologies
- Capital markets and funding strategy education

## 7. AMENDMENT AND REVIEW

This Charter may be amended by the Board of Managers. The Charter shall be reviewed annually and updated to reflect market conditions and regulatory requirements.

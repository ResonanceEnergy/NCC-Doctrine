# INVESTMENT COMMITTEE (IC) CHARTER

**Faraday Financial Corporation LLC**  
**Effective Date:** [Insert Date]  
**Approved By:** Board of Managers  

## 1. PURPOSE AND AUTHORITY

The Investment Committee (IC) is established to oversee the development, approval, and management of all quantitative trading strategies and investment activities. The IC shall ensure that all strategies align with the Company's risk-adjusted return objectives while maintaining compliance with regulatory requirements and internal risk limits.

## 2. COMPOSITION

### 2.1 Membership
- Chair: Chief Investment Officer
- Members: Chief Risk Officer, Head of Quantitative Research, Regional Trading Heads, and external quant expert
- Secretary: Designated by the Chair

### 2.2 Term
Members serve at the pleasure of the Board of Managers.

### 2.3 Expertise
Committee members shall have expertise in quantitative finance, risk management, and trading execution.

## 3. RESPONSIBILITIES AND DUTIES

### 3.1 Strategy Approval
- Review and approve all new trading strategies and models
- Establish strategy-specific parameters and limits
- Monitor strategy performance and evolution
- Approve strategy modifications and decommissioning

### 3.2 Portfolio Construction
- Oversee portfolio allocation across strategies and asset classes
- Establish diversification requirements and concentration limits
- Monitor portfolio risk exposures and correlations
- Approve tactical asset allocation decisions

### 3.3 Performance Management
- Review risk-adjusted returns and Sharpe ratios
- Analyze strategy contribution to overall portfolio performance
- Establish performance benchmarks and attribution analysis
- Recommend strategy scaling and de-risking actions

### 3.4 Market Regime Analysis
- Assess current market conditions and regime changes
- Adjust strategy exposures based on market environment
- Evaluate macroeconomic impacts on strategy performance
- Coordinate with risk management for regime-specific adjustments

## 4. MEETINGS AND PROCEDURES

### 4.1 Regular Meetings
- Weekly meetings for strategy review and performance analysis
- Monthly deep-dive sessions for new strategy evaluation
- Special meetings for urgent strategy or market issues

### 4.2 Quorum and Voting
- Quorum: Majority of members
- Decisions: Majority vote for standard approvals, supermajority for major strategy changes

### 4.3 Decision Rights
- Final authority for strategy approval and position limits
- Escalation to Board for systemic strategy changes or major portfolio shifts

### 4.4 Documentation
- All strategy decisions and rationales documented
- Meeting minutes maintained with action items tracked

## 5. REPORTING AND OVERSIGHT

### 5.1 Board Reporting
- Weekly performance dashboard and risk metrics
- Monthly strategy review and portfolio analysis
- Quarterly strategic investment plan updates

### 5.2 Metrics and KPIs
- Sharpe ratio: >2.0 target
- Alpha persistence: >80% win rate
- Strategy approval cycle time: <30 days
- Portfolio diversification: Within defined limits

### 5.3 Annual Review
- Investment strategy effectiveness assessment
- Market condition analysis and adaptation
- Process improvements and technology upgrades

## 6. RESOURCES AND SUPPORT

### 6.1 Research Team
- Dedicated quantitative research and strategy development team
- Access to backtesting platforms and market data
- Performance analytics and risk modeling tools

### 6.2 Technology
- Real-time portfolio monitoring systems
- Strategy simulation and optimization tools
- Performance attribution and risk analytics platforms

### 6.3 Training
- Ongoing education in quantitative methods and market analysis
- Strategy development workshops
- Risk management training for investment decisions

## 7. AMENDMENT AND REVIEW

This Charter may be amended by the Board of Managers. The Charter shall be reviewed annually and updated to reflect market conditions and regulatory changes.

# MODEL VALIDATION COMMITTEE (MVC) CHARTER

**Faraday Financial Corporation LLC**  
**Effective Date:** [Insert Date]  
**Approved By:** Board of Managers  

## 1. PURPOSE AND AUTHORITY

The Model Validation Committee (MVC) is established to ensure the integrity, accuracy, and reliability of all quantitative models, algorithms, and trading strategies used by the Company. The MVC shall oversee the development, testing, validation, and ongoing monitoring of models to ensure they perform as expected and comply with regulatory requirements.

## 2. COMPOSITION

### 2.1 Membership
- Chair: Head of Quantitative Research
- Members: Chief Risk Officer, Chief Technology Officer, senior quantitative researchers, and external validation expert
- Secretary: Designated by the Chair

### 2.2 Term
Members serve at the pleasure of the Board of Managers.

### 2.3 Expertise
Committee members shall have expertise in quantitative finance, statistical modeling, machine learning, and model risk management.

## 3. RESPONSIBILITIES AND DUTIES

### 3.1 Model Development Oversight
- Review model development methodologies and assumptions
- Assess data quality and preprocessing procedures
- Evaluate model architecture and algorithmic approaches
- Ensure proper documentation and version control

### 3.2 Validation and Testing
- Oversee backtesting procedures and out-of-sample testing
- Review walk-forward analysis and cross-validation results
- Assess model stability and robustness across market regimes
- Evaluate performance attribution and risk decomposition

### 3.3 Performance Monitoring
- Monitor ongoing model performance and prediction accuracy
- Track model drift and concept drift detection
- Review periodic revalidation and recalibration procedures
- Assess model contribution to overall portfolio performance

### 3.4 Regulatory Compliance
- Ensure models meet regulatory validation requirements
- Prepare documentation for regulatory examinations
- Maintain audit trails for model changes and decisions
- Coordinate with external auditors and regulators

### 3.5 Model Risk Management
- Identify and assess model risk exposures
- Develop fallback procedures and model redundancy
- Review incident response for model failures
- Establish model governance and change management procedures

## 4. MEETINGS AND PROCEDURES

### 4.1 Regular Meetings
- Monthly model performance and validation review
- Quarterly comprehensive model assessment
- Special meetings for new model approvals or significant issues

### 4.2 Quorum and Voting
- Quorum: Majority of members
- Decisions: Majority vote for standard validations, supermajority for critical model decisions

### 4.3 Decision Rights
- Authority to approve or reject new models and strategies
- Power to require model recalibration or decommissioning
- Escalation to Board for systemic model risk issues

### 4.4 Documentation
- All validation results and model assessments documented
- Change logs and version histories maintained
- Regulatory submissions and audit responses tracked

## 5. REPORTING AND OVERSIGHT

### 5.1 Board Reporting
- Monthly model performance and validation status
- Quarterly comprehensive model risk assessment
- Immediate notification of material model issues or failures

### 5.2 Metrics and KPIs
- Model accuracy: >60% prediction accuracy target
- Backtesting performance: >95% confidence intervals
- Out-of-sample validation: Consistent with in-sample results
- Regulatory compliance: 100% audit readiness

### 5.3 Annual Review
- Model validation framework effectiveness assessment
- Technology and methodology updates
- Regulatory requirement changes and adaptations

## 6. RESOURCES AND SUPPORT

### 6.1 Validation Team
- Dedicated model validation and quantitative research team
- Access to independent data sources and testing environments
- Statistical and computational resources for validation work

### 6.2 Technology
- Model validation platforms and testing frameworks
- Performance monitoring and alerting systems
- Version control and documentation systems

### 6.3 Training
- Advanced quantitative methods and validation techniques
- Regulatory requirements for model risk management
- Statistical testing and hypothesis evaluation

## 7. AMENDMENT AND REVIEW

This Charter may be amended by the Board of Managers. The Charter shall be reviewed annually and updated to reflect technological advancements and regulatory changes.

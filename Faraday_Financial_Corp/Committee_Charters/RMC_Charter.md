# RISK MANAGEMENT COMMITTEE (RMC) CHARTER

**Faraday Financial Corporation LLC**  
**Effective Date:** [Insert Date]  
**Approved By:** Board of Managers  

## 1. PURPOSE AND AUTHORITY

The Risk Management Committee (RMC) is established to oversee the identification, measurement, monitoring, and control of all financial and operational risks across the Company. The RMC shall ensure that risk-taking activities align with the Company's risk appetite and regulatory requirements while maintaining financial stability and operational resilience.

## 2. COMPOSITION

### 2.1 Membership
- Chair: Chief Risk Officer
- Members: Chief Investment Officer, Chief Technology Officer, Head of Quantitative Research, and compliance lead
- Secretary: Designated by the Chair

### 2.2 Term
Members serve at the pleasure of the Board of Managers.

### 2.3 Expertise
Committee members shall have expertise in quantitative risk management, regulatory compliance, and financial modeling.

## 3. RESPONSIBILITIES AND DUTIES

### 3.1 Risk Framework
- Establish enterprise risk management framework and policies
- Define risk appetite and tolerance limits
- Develop risk measurement methodologies and models
- Monitor adherence to risk limits across all activities

### 3.2 Market Risk Management
- Oversee value-at-risk (VaR) models and stress testing
- Monitor position limits and concentration risks
- Assess market liquidity and funding risks
- Evaluate counterparty and credit exposures

### 3.3 Operational Risk Management
- Identify and assess operational risk exposures
- Develop business continuity and disaster recovery plans
- Monitor technology and cybersecurity risks
- Review incident response and crisis management protocols

### 3.4 Regulatory Compliance
- Ensure compliance with securities regulations and reporting requirements
- Coordinate regulatory examinations and audits
- Monitor changes in regulatory requirements
- Maintain compliance training and awareness programs

### 3.5 Stress Testing and Scenario Analysis
- Conduct regular stress testing of portfolios and strategies
- Develop adverse scenario analysis and contingency plans
- Assess impact of macroeconomic events and market shocks
- Review model risk and validation procedures

## 4. MEETINGS AND PROCEDURES

### 4.1 Regular Meetings
- Weekly risk dashboard review and limit monitoring
- Monthly comprehensive risk assessment
- Quarterly stress testing and scenario analysis review

### 4.2 Quorum and Voting
- Quorum: Majority of members
- Decisions: Majority vote for standard risk policy matters, unanimous for emergency risk actions

### 4.3 Decision Rights
- Authority to adjust risk limits within approved ranges
- Escalation to Board for breaches of risk appetite or regulatory violations
- Emergency authority to halt trading activities in crisis situations

### 4.4 Documentation
- All risk decisions and limit changes documented
- Stress test results and scenario analyses maintained
- Regulatory communications and compliance actions logged

## 5. REPORTING AND OVERSIGHT

### 5.1 Board Reporting
- Daily risk dashboard with key metrics and limit breaches
- Weekly risk report with portfolio exposures
- Monthly comprehensive risk management report
- Quarterly regulatory compliance and audit updates

### 5.2 Metrics and KPIs
- Risk limit breaches: Zero tolerance
- Regulatory violations: Zero material violations
- Stress test coverage: 100% of portfolio
- Risk model accuracy: >95% backtesting performance

### 5.3 Annual Review
- Risk framework effectiveness assessment
- Regulatory landscape review and updates
- Technology and methodology enhancements

## 6. RESOURCES AND SUPPORT

### 6.1 Risk Team
- Dedicated quantitative risk management team
- Access to market data and risk analytics platforms
- Independent validation and audit functions

### 6.2 Technology
- Real-time risk monitoring and alerting systems
- Stress testing and scenario analysis platforms
- Regulatory reporting and compliance automation tools

### 6.3 Training
- Risk management methodology training
- Regulatory compliance workshops
- Crisis simulation exercises

## 7. AMENDMENT AND REVIEW

This Charter may be amended by the Board of Managers. The Charter shall be reviewed annually and updated to reflect regulatory changes and market developments.

## PART II: CORE CONCEPTS EXPLAINED

### What is Paramagnetism?

**Definition**: **Paramagnetism** is the property of a material to be **weakly attracted to magnetic fields**. Unlike ferromagnetism (strong permanent magnetism like iron magnets), paramagnetic materials only show magnetism in the presence of an external field and lose it when the field is removed.

**Atomic Basis**:
- Atoms with **unpaired electrons** in outer shells are paramagnetic
- These unpaired electrons have magnetic moments (spin angular momentum)
- External magnetic field aligns these moments → net attraction

**Paramagnetic Elements** (relevant to agriculture/geology):
- **Iron (Fe)**: Most important, found in basalt, granite, clay, organic matter
- **Manganese (Mn)**: Critical trace element, highly paramagnetic
- **Aluminum (Al)**: Paramagnetic, abundant in clays and feldspars
- **Magnesium (Mg)**: Paramagnetic, essential for chlorophyll
- **Oxygen (O₂)**: Paramagnetic gas (unusual for molecule)

**Diamagnetism** (Opposite):
- Materials **weakly repelled** by magnetic fields
- All atoms have diamagnetism (from orbital electron motion)
- Examples: Water (H2O), quartz sand (SiO2), organic carbon compounds
- **Key**: Most living tissue and water are diamagnetic, so paramagnetic environment creates contrast/gradient

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### What is Stellite?

**Stellite** is a family of cobalt-chromium-tungsten-carbon alloys renowned for:
- **Extreme wear resistance** (10-100Ã— better than stainless steel)
- **Corrosion immunity** (superior to 316 stainless in seawater, abrasive slurries)
- **High-temperature stability** (maintains hardness to 800Â°C+)
- **Cavitation resistance** (used in ship propellers, hydraulic turbines for 100+ years)

**Typical Composition** (Stellite 6, most common grade):
- 28-32% Chromium (corrosion resistance)
- 4.5% Tungsten (hardness, wear resistance)
- 1% Carbon (hardness, carbide formation)
- 2.5% Nickel, Iron, Silicon (balance)
- Balance Cobalt (matrix, high-temperature strength)

### 5. Penstock Interior (LOW-MEDIUM PRIORITY)

**Current Challenge**:
- Penstock transports water from intake to turbine (50-500 meters typical)
- Interior surface friction reduces pressure (head loss = efficiency loss)
- Abrasive sediment erodes interior over decades (increases roughness â†’ more friction)
- Current materials: HDPE (smooth, low-cost), steel (durable but rougher), fiberglass (good balance)

**Stellite Solution**: **Stellite Spray Coating or Lining in High-Wear Zones**

**Design Option A: Strategic Stellite Coating**
- Base penstock: HDPE or steel (standard, cost-effective)
- Stellite spray coating: 0.5-2mm thick in high-wear zones (bends, elbows, inlet/outlet)
- Method: Thermal spray (HVOF - High-Velocity Oxy-Fuel) or plasma spray
- Coverage: 5-10% of penstock length (only critical zones)

**Design Option B: Full Stellite Lining (Exotic/Extreme Applications)**
- Entire penstock interior coated with Stellite (0.5-1mm thick)
- Only for extreme sediment environments (volcanic ash, mining slurry, glacier-fed streams)
- Cost prohibitive for most applications ($10k-50k+ depending on length)

**Performance Benefits**:
- **Friction reduction**: Stellite can be polished to 8-16 Ra (mirror finish, near-zero friction)
- **Erosion immunity**: Decades of sediment flow cause minimal roughness increase
- **Efficiency preservation**: Maintains low head loss for 30-50 years (vs. increasing loss as HDPE/steel roughens)

**Cost Analysis**:
- Stellite spray coating: $50-200 per meter (high-wear zones only, 5-10% of length)
- Penstock length: 50-500 meters typical = $2,500-10,000 for strategic coating
- Full lining: $200-1,000 per meter (prohibitive except extreme cases)
- **ROI**: **MARGINAL** - Head loss increases slowly even with HDPE/steel (1-3% over 30 years), Stellite savings minimal

**Decision**: **LOW PRIORITY** for most applications. Consider Stellite penstock coating only if:
1. Extreme sediment environment (volcanic ash >10g/L, mining slurry)

### TIG Welding Stellite: Process Details

**Equipment Required**:
- TIG welder (GTAW - Gas Tungsten Arc Welding): $1k-5k (standard shop tool)
- Argon gas (shielding): $50-100 per cylinder (lasts 20-50 hours welding)
- Stellite 6 welding rod: $60-150 per pound (AWS ERCoCr-A classification)
- Tungsten electrode: 2% thoriated or lanthanated ($10-30, reusable)
- Grinding wheel: Silicon carbide or diamond (for hard materials, $20-100)

**Welding Parameters** (Stellite 6 on 316 Stainless Steel):
- Current: 80-150 amps (DC, electrode negative)
- Voltage: 12-18 volts
- Travel speed: 3-6 inches/minute (slow, controlled)
- Argon flow: 15-25 CFH (cubic feet per hour)
- Preheat: 150-300Â°F (prevents cracking due to thermal shock)
- Interpass temperature: <600Â°F (multi-pass welding, maintain low heat input)

**Overlay Thickness**:
- Single pass: 0.5-1.5mm (thin overlay, good for valve seats)
- Multi-pass: 2-5mm (thick overlay, for turbine blades, nozzle throats)
- Final machine: Grind to final dimensions (leave 0.2-0.5mm stock for finishing)

**Surface Finish**:
- As-welded: 125-250 Ra (rough, needs grinding)
- After grinding: 32-63 Ra (smooth, acceptable for most applications)
- After lapping/polishing: 4-16 Ra (mirror finish, best for seals, nozzles, bearings)

**Skill Level**:
- Experienced TIG welder: 20-40 hours training to master Stellite (harder than stainless, but not exotic)
- Quality control: Visual inspection (no cracks, uniform bead), hardness test (38-45 HRC), dimensional check

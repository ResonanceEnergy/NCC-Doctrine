### Visual Composition

#### Circle Format
- **Outer border:** Dark navy or forest green border (3–5 pt thickness); represents structure and permanence
- **Background fill:** Gradient or solid color TBD (see color palette below)
- **Diameter:** Scalable; design should work from 0.5" to 8"+ without loss of clarity

#### Sky Section (Top 40%)
- **Element:** Soft blue gradient, lighter at horizon
- **Watermark:** Light infinity symbol (∞) subtly embedded in sky (very faint, 10–15% opacity)
  - Represents infinite renewable energy, timelessness, cyclical nature of water
  - Positioned center-top, slightly toward left side
- **Purpose:** Connection to sky, air, and boundless possibility

#### Landscape Layers (Middle 40%)

**Mountain Range (Top of landscape section)**
- **Style:** Geometric, simplified silhouette (2–3 peaks)
- **Placement:** Centered, spanning roughly the middle-upper landscape area
- **Purpose:** Bold, iconic presence; reference to Canadian Rocky Mountain geography and elevation (water source)

**Hill Range (Below mountains)**
- **Style:** Gentle, rounded hills (2–3 hills)
- **Placement:** Below mountains, slightly overlapping
- **Color:** Slightly darker green or earth tone than sky
- **Purpose:** Transition from dramatic to pastoral; represents foothills and accessible terrain

**Plains (Below hills)**
- **Style:** Simple horizontal lines or grass texture
- **Color:** Light tan, cream, or light green
- **Placement:** Between hills and river
- **Height:** Occupies roughly 15–20% of logo
- **Purpose:** Open space, accessibility, agricultural heritage

#### Water Section (Bottom 30%)

**River**
- **Style:** Flowing lines with organic curves (not straight)
- **Color:** Blue or teal gradient; darker/richer blue than sky
- **Placement:** Bottom third of logo, spanning full width
- **Texture:** Suggest movement with parallel flowing lines or ripple pattern
- **Height:** Approximately 30% of logo diameter
- **Purpose:** Core water resource; life-giving force; dynamic and flowing

#### Industrial Icons (Left & Right Sides)

**Left Side: Historic Waterwheel**
- **Style:** Simplified geometric representation of waterwheel (8–12 buckets visible)
- **Placement:** Left side of logo, integrated into landscape (wheel base at river level, top of wheel in hills/plains area)
- **Size:** ~25% of logo diameter
- **Color:** Warm metallic tone (bronze, copper, or warm brown) to suggest wood and iron
- **Details:** Visible spokes and hub; clear bucket geometry; artistic but recognizable
- **Purpose:** Core technology heritage; waterwheel symbolizes conversion of water energy to mechanical power

**Right Side: Historic Mill Building**
- **Style:** Simplified silhouette of windmill or grain mill (tower or post mill)
- **Placement:** Right side of logo, integrated into landscape (base at river level, top in hill/mountain area)
- **Size:** ~25% of logo diameter
- **Color:** Similar warm tone to waterwheel, slightly varied to create visual distinction
- **Details:** Visible sails or window openings; recognizable mill structure; artistic integration with landscape
- **Purpose:** Complement to waterwheel; mill symbolizes human innovation and craft

#### Integration & Balance
- **Symmetry:** Roughly balanced left (waterwheel) and right (mill), with landscape centered
- **Overlapping:** Waterwheel and mill integrate smoothly with landscape layers (not floating on top)
- **Negative space:** Sky occupies ~40%, landscape ~40%, river ~20%; leaves breathing room
- **Visual hierarchy:** Mountains most prominent; waterwheel and mill secondary; river foundational

## Visual Applications

### Primary Uses
- **Website header/hero**
- **Business cards & letterhead**
- **Product packaging**
- **Social media profiles & posts**
- **Certifications & seals**
- **Vehicle wraps & signage**

### Variations

**Full Color (Primary)**
- Use complete color palette; all landscape and sky elements visible
- Best for digital, print, professional contexts

**Monochrome (Secondary)**
- Black or navy version for fax, simple printing, professional documents
- White version on dark backgrounds

**Flat Variation**
- Simplified version without gradients; solid colors only
- For small sizes, app icons, embroidery

**Linear/Line Art Variation**
- Outline version; useful for laser engraving, stamping, embossing

### Sizing & Spacing

- **Minimum size:** 0.5 inches (print) / 100 pixels (digital); detail should remain clear
- **Clear space:** Maintain 10% of logo diameter as padding around the circle
- **Scaling:** Logo should scale proportionally; do not stretch or distort
- **Background:** Works on white, light backgrounds best; also effective on dark navy or forest green

## Brand Guidelines

### Logo Usage

**Do:**
- ✅ Use full-color version on white or light backgrounds
- ✅ Use monochrome version on darker backgrounds
- ✅ Maintain clear space around logo
- ✅ Scale proportionally
- ✅ Place on brand-approved background colors

**Don't:**
- ❌ Rotate the logo (water/river should always be at bottom)
- ❌ Distort or stretch the logo
- ❌ Add drop shadows or complex effects that weren't in original design
- ❌ Change colors without approval
- ❌ Use logo upside-down or reversed without specific guidance
- ❌ Outline the logo with low-contrast colors

### Logo + Text Combinations

**Horizontal lockup:**
- Logo (circle) on left
- Company name & tagline stacked on right
- Minimum spacing: 20 pixels (digital) or 0.25" (print) between circle and text

**Stacked lockup:**
- Logo (circle) at top, centered
- Company name below, centered
- Suitable for narrow formats (social avatars, small badges)

**Circle only:**
- Use standalone circle for app icons, favicons, social avatars, profile pictures

## Color Accessibility & Contrast

### WCAG Compliance
- Navy border on blue sky: Contrast ratio ~3:1 (acceptable for graphics, not text)
- Water blue on forest green: Contrast ratio ~2:1 (acceptable for design elements)
- Text (if any) should maintain 4.5:1 contrast (AA) or 7:1 (AAA) per WCAG standards

### Colorblind Considerations
- Logo does not rely solely on color differentiation (shape and position also convey meaning)
- Waterwheel (left) and mill (right) positioned distinctly; not distinguished only by color
- River (bottom) clearly water-like in form; not just blue

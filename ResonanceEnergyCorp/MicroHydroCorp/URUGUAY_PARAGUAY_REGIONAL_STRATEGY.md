### Legal Entity Structure Options

**Option 1: Sociedad Anónima (S.A.) - Corporation**
- **Equivalent to**: C-Corp (USA), Ltd (UK), S.A. (Europe)
- **Requirements**: 
  * Minimum 2 shareholders (can be individuals or companies)
  * Minimum capital: $0 (no minimum, but $5,000-$10,000 typical for credibility)
  * Board of directors (3+ directors)
  * Registered address in Uruguay (virtual office = $50-100/month)
- **Taxation**: 
  * Corporate income tax: 25% (standard rate)
  * **BUT** can establish Free Trade Zone (Zona Franca) entity = 0% corporate tax on international income
  * Dividend withholding tax: 7% (paid to foreign shareholders)
- **Timeline**: 2-4 weeks to incorporate
- **Cost**: $1,500-$3,000 (legal fees + notary + registry)

**Option 2: Zona Franca (Free Trade Zone) Entity - S.A. de Zona Franca**
- **Purpose**: Companies serving international markets (NOT domestic Uruguay)
- **Tax Benefits**:
  * **0% corporate income tax** on international sales (exports, services to foreign clients)
  * **0% VAT** on imports/exports
  * **0% dividend tax** to foreign shareholders
  * **0% wealth tax** on company assets
  * **ONLY** pay Uruguayan tax on Uruguayan domestic sales (if any)
- **Requirements**:
  * Must operate from designated Zona Franca location (office park near Montevideo, Colonia)
  * Office rent: $300-800/month (furnished, high-speed internet, shared facilities)
  * Minimum 5 employees working from Zona Franca office (can include founder, engineers, admin)
  * Annual audit required (cost: $2,000-$5,000/year)
  * Export >50% of revenue (first 3 years = flexible, then must prove export focus)
- **Timeline**: 3-6 months (Zona Franca application + incorporation)
- **Cost**: $5,000-$10,000 initial (application, legal, setup) + $15k-25k/year (5 employees + office + audit)

**Recommendation**: **Zona Franca S.A.**
- **Why**: If MicroHydro sells 90%+ of systems to Paraguay, Bolivia, Kenya, Nepal (international), Uruguay domestic = <10%
- **Tax savings**: On $5M revenue/year, save $1.25M in corporate tax (25% × $5M) + $350k dividend tax (7% × $5M) = **$1.6M/year saved**
- **ROI on Zona Franca cost**: Pay $25k/year to save $1.6M/year = **64× return on investment**

### Transfer Pricing Model (Tax Optimization)

**How It Works**:
1. **Uruguay S.A. owns all IP** (patents, designs, φ-optimization algorithms)
2. **Paraguay S.R.L. licenses IP** from Uruguay S.A. (licensing fee = 5-8% of Paraguay revenue)
3. **Paraguay manufactures & sells** systems to customers (revenue in Paraguay S.R.L.)
4. **Paraguay pays licensing fee** to Uruguay S.A. (transfer revenue to Uruguay tax-free structure)
5. **Uruguay S.A. receives licensing income** tax-free (Zona Franca = 0% on international income)

**Example** (Year 3 scenario):
- **Paraguay S.R.L. revenue**: $3M (30 systems × $100k average)
- **Licensing fee** (7% of revenue): $210k paid to Uruguay S.A.
- **Paraguay taxable income**: $3M - $210k - $2M costs = $590k profit
- **Paraguay tax** (10%): $59k
- **Uruguay licensing income**: $210k (0% tax)
- **Total tax paid**: $59k (effective rate = 1.9% of $3M revenue)

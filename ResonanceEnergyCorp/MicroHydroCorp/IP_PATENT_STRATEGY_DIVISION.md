## PART VI: RISK MITIGATION & DEFENSIVE STRATEGY

### Risk #1: Patent Infringement Lawsuit (Against Us)

**Scenario**: Competitor claims our œÜ-Turbine infringes their existing turbine patent

**Mitigation**:
- **Freedom-to-Operate (FTO) Analysis**: Before filing patent, search for blocking patents (if found, design around or acquire license)
- **Invalidity Insurance**: Purchase patent infringement defense insurance ($5k-20k/year, covers legal costs up to $1M-5M)
- **Strong Patent Attorney**: Hire attorney with litigation experience (they draft claims to withstand challenges)
- **Prior Art File**: Maintain comprehensive prior art documentation (proves our independent invention, no willful infringement)

**Budget**: $10k-30k/year (FTO searches, insurance, attorney retainer)

### Risk #2: Competitors Copy Our Technology (Before Patent Granted)

**Scenario**: Provisional patent filed (not yet published), competitor sees our product at trade show, reverse engineers, files own patent

**Mitigation**:
- **Rapid PCT Conversion**: Convert provisional to PCT within 6-9 months (vs. waiting 12 months), locks in priority date faster
- **Publication Strategy**: Publish detailed technical white paper AFTER provisional filing (creates prior art, prevents competitor patents)
- **Trade Secret Hybrid**: Patent visible innovations (turbine blade geometry), keep manufacturing processes as trade secrets (e.g., basalt fiber layup technique)
- **Non-Disclosure Agreements**: All customers, partners, suppliers sign NDAs before seeing proprietary technology

### Risk #3: Patent Granted but Narrow Claims (Easy to Design Around)

**Scenario**: Examiner rejects broad claims, only grants narrow claims covering very specific embodiment

**Mitigation**:
- **Continuation Applications**: File continuation patent applications with broader/alternative claims (leverages same priority date)
- **Multiple Provisional Filings**: File 2-3 provisional patents for same innovation (different claim approaches), choose strongest for PCT
- **Experienced Attorney**: Patent attorney skilled in "claims judo" (reframing rejections, finding unexpected angles)

### Risk #4: Patent Expires Before Revenue Peaks

**Scenario**: 20-year patent term expires in 2046, but market adoption is slow (licensing revenue doesn't peak until 2050)

**Mitigation**:
- **Continuation Patents**: File continuation/divisional applications every 3-5 years (can extend effective term to 25-30 years via patent family)
- **Trade Secrets for Longevity**: Patent core innovations (turbine geometry), keep improvements as trade secrets (indefinite protection)
- **Brand Dominance**: By patent expiry, brand = market leader (patents = temporary moat, brand = permanent moat)

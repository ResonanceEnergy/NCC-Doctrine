## Production Ramp Schedule

### Phase 1: Pre-Production (Months 1-2)
- Finalize supplier contracts and purchase orders
- Set up manufacturing lines and tooling
- Conduct First Article Inspection (FAI)
- Train production team on assembly and QC procedures
- **Target output:** 0 units (validation phase)

### Phase 2: Soft Launch (Months 3-4)
- Begin production at 10% of capacity
- Build first production units with intensive inspection
- Validate supply chain and assembly process
- Gather feedback and make adjustments
- **Target output:** [X units]

### Phase 3: Ramp (Months 5-8)
- Scale to 50% capacity by Month 5
- Scale to 100% capacity by Month 8
- Stabilize quality and reduce inspection overhead
- Optimize supplier lead times and inventory
- **Target output:** [X units/month] by end

### Phase 4: Steady State (Months 9-12)
- Maintain full-capacity production
- Continuous improvement (Kaizen)
- Monitor quality, cost, and delivery KPIs
- Plan for next product version or scale-up
- **Target output:** [X units/month] sustained

## Decision Gates & Go/No-Go Criteria

### Gate 1: End of Pre-Production (Month 2)
- [ ] FAI completed and approved
- [ ] All suppliers contracted and POs placed
- [ ] Manufacturing line ready and tested
- [ ] Quality procedures documented and training complete
- [ ] Supply chain logistics validated
- **Decision:** Proceed to Soft Launch (Phase 2) or re-plan

### Gate 2: End of Soft Launch (Month 4)
- [ ] [X] production units completed without critical defects
- [ ] First-pass yield ≥[Y]%
- [ ] Customer feedback positive (NPS > [X])
- [ ] Cost per unit within ±10% of target
- [ ] Supply chain delivering on schedule
- **Decision:** Proceed to Ramp (Phase 3) or extend soft launch

### Gate 3: Mid-Ramp (Month 6)
- [ ] Ramped to 50% capacity as planned
- [ ] Manufacturing efficiency improving (trend positive)
- [ ] Quality stable and predictable
- [ ] Customer orders and pipeline healthy
- [ ] Cost tracking to target
- **Decision:** Continue to full ramp or slow down / troubleshoot

### Gate 4: Full Capacity (Month 8)
- [ ] Reached 100% production capacity
- [ ] Manufacturing metrics all green
- [ ] Quality fully stabilized
- [ ] Supply chain robust and scalable
- [ ] Financial targets on track
- **Decision:** Maintain steady state or scale up further

### Gate 5: Year-End Review (Month 12)
- [ ] All 12-month KPI targets met or exceeded
- [ ] Customer satisfaction and retention strong
- [ ] Profitability targets achieved
- [ ] Lessons learned captured
- [ ] Next product version / scale roadmap clear
- **Decision:** Plan next phase (v2 product, new market, geographic expansion, etc.)

## Escalation Path

### Level 1: Weekly Sync Issues
- **Owner:** Functional lead (e.g., Manufacturing, Procurement)
- **Action:** Re-plan locally, update dependency tracking
- **Escalate if:** Not resolved within 2 days

### Level 2: Cross-Functional Issues
- **Owner:** Program Manager
- **Action:** Coordinate across functions, identify trade-offs
- **Escalate if:** Not resolved within 1 week

### Level 3: Strategic Issues
- **Owner:** VP / Senior leadership
- **Action:** Assess impact on roadmap, reallocate resources, communicate to board
- **Escalate if:** Risk to delivery, budget, or customer commitments

## Communication & Cadence

### Weekly (Every Monday or designated day)
- Cross-functional standup (30 min)
  - Manufacturing status
  - Supply chain status
  - Quality issues
  - Customer escalations
  - Next week risks and priorities
- Attendees: All functional owners listed above

### Bi-Weekly (Every other week)
- Detailed functional reviews (60 min each)
  - Manufacturing deep dive
  - Quality & defect root cause analysis
  - Supply chain / procurement pipeline
  - Finance & cost tracking
  - Customer feedback and learnings

### Monthly
- Executive dashboard review
  - All KPIs vs. targets
  - Go/no-go gates status
  - Financial summary
  - Risk register update
  - Lessons learned and process improvements

### Quarterly
- Board update (if required)
  - Executive summary of progress
  - Key achievements and challenges
  - Financial results
  - Strategic implications

## Continuous Improvement & Kaizen

### Monthly Improvement Cycle
1. **Identify:** Frontline team suggests improvements (yield, cost, quality, speed)
2. **Evaluate:** Engineering and operations assess feasibility and ROI
3. **Implement:** Quick experiments or process changes (PDCA)
4. **Standardize:** If successful, update work instructions and train team
5. **Document:** Add to standard work and lessons learned log

### Continuous Improvement Targets
- Reduce manufacturing cost 5% per year
- Improve first-pass yield 2% per year
- Reduce lead time by 10% in first year
- Capture 3-5 improvement ideas per employee per year
